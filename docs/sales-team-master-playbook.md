# **4. What LWA / IWA Is**

## **Simple explanation**

**LWA / IWA is an AI-powered content repurposing tool.**

It helps turn **one long-form video** into **multiple short-form pieces of content** faster.

## **What it does**

LWA helps users:

* analyze long-form videos
* identify strong clip-worthy moments
* generate hooks
* generate captions
* create descriptions
* provide timestamps
* make it easier to turn content into shorts, reels, and clips

## **What problem it solves**

Most creators and brands already have content.
The real bottleneck is:

* editing takes too long
* repurposing is inconsistent
* teams are overloaded
* people do not know what section to clip
* they do not know what hook or caption to use
* content output is lower than it should be

LWA solves that bottleneck.

## **What we are really selling**

We are not just selling features.

We are selling:

* more output
* less manual work
* more consistency
* more leverage
* more visibility
* more monetization potential

# **7. Best-Fit Buyer vs Bad-Fit Buyer**

## **A strong buyer usually**

* already creates content
* wants to grow
* understands the value of posting consistently
* has long-form content that can be reused
* wants faster production
* is tired of doing everything manually
* has a team or wants to scale like one

## **A weak buyer usually**

* does not make content at all
* barely posts and does not care
* wants magical results with no effort
* has no real content workflow
* is not serious about growth
* is only curious with no intent

## **Rule**

Do not spend too much energy trying to convince people who are not real fits.

Go where the pain is obvious.

# **12. The Sales Process**

## **Step 1: Find the right people**

Prospect for:

* active creators
* people posting video content regularly
* businesses building a brand online
* agencies handling content for clients
* editors and clipping teams
* managers of creator brands

## **Step 2: Open the conversation**

Do not send giant paragraphs.

Keep it simple.

### **Cold DM openers**

* **“Quick question, are you currently repurposing your long-form content into short-form at scale?”**
* **“How are you currently handling clips from your long-form content?”**
* **“Do you have a system for turning one video into multiple short-form posts?”**
* **“Are you still doing your clips manually?”**
* **“How are you currently turning podcasts/videos into shorts or reels?”**

## **Step 3: Diagnose pain**

Ask:

* “How long does clipping take you right now?”
* “How often are you posting short-form content?”
* “Who handles editing right now?”
* “Do you feel like you’re getting enough value out of each long-form video?”
* “What part of the workflow slows you down most?”

## **Step 4: Position LWA**

Once pain is clear, connect it directly.

Say:
**“That’s exactly where LWA helps. It makes it easier to turn long-form content into multiple short-form assets faster, without having to figure everything out from scratch manually.”**

## **Step 5: Move to the offer**

Say:
**“If it helps, I can show you how people are using it to get more short-form output out of the content they already have.”**

## **Step 6: Ask for the next step**

This could be:

* try the product
* walk through the offer
* continue the conversation
* test it on one piece of content
* start with the entry offer

# **17. Objection Handling**

## **Objection: “I can already do this myself.”**

**Response:**
**Absolutely. A lot of people can. The real question is whether doing it manually is slowing down your output more than it should. LWA is really about speed, consistency, and scale.**

## **Objection: “I already have an editor.”**

**Response:**
**That actually makes sense. LWA does not have to replace your editor. It can help your editor or team move faster and squeeze more output from the same content.**

## **Objection: “I’m not sure I need this.”**

**Response:**
**Fair. The easiest filter is simple: if long-form content matters to you and you want more short-form output from it, then this is relevant.**

## **Objection: “I’m not posting enough yet.”**

**Response:**
**That’s exactly why this can help. It reduces the friction between creating content and actually publishing enough of it consistently.**

## **Objection: “Price.”**

**Response:**
**Totally fair. Most people compare this to the cost of time, manual clipping, slower posting, or extra editing workload. It usually becomes a workflow efficiency decision more than just a software cost question.**

## **Objection: “Let me think about it.”**

**Response:**
**Absolutely. Usually what people are really weighing is whether solving the bottleneck now matters enough. Happy to answer anything that would make the decision easier.**

## **Objection: “Send me more info.”**

**Response:**
**Absolutely. I’ll keep it simple and relevant. The main value is helping turn long-form content into more short-form output faster, so you can post more consistently without doing everything manually.**

# **19. What Reps Should Say vs What Reps Should Avoid**

## **Say this**

* “This helps you get more output from content you already made.”
* “This helps reduce the manual workload.”
* “This helps creators and teams post more consistently.”

## **Avoid saying this**

* “This will make you instantly go viral.”
* “This guarantees money.”
* “This replaces all editing forever.”
* “This does everything automatically with zero work.”
* “This is magic.”
* anything exaggerated, dishonest, or unrealistic

# **21. Using Whop Properly**

Reps need to understand the platform, but they should stay in their lane.

## **Reps should use Whop to**

* understand the product page
* understand how customers join
* understand offers and positioning
* check communication areas if authorized
* see how members move through the platform
* help direct prospects to the right offer or page

## **Reps should NOT change without approval**

* payments
* checkout settings
* team permissions
* company settings
* developer tools
* anything involving billing, payouts, or infrastructure
* sensitive access controls

## **Rule**

If you are not sure whether you should touch something, do **not** touch it. Ask first.
